\section{Theoretical Background}

\subsection{Degrees of Freedom and Transformation}

Each two-dimensional node is represented with translational and rotational degrees of freedom:
\[
q_n = [u_x,\ u_y,\ r_z]^T .
\]
For a two-node frame element, the local element vector is
\[
q_e = [u_{x,i}, u_{y,i}, r_{z,i}, u_{x,j}, u_{y,j}, r_{z,j}]^T .
\]
Element local and global quantities are connected through the transformation matrix \(T\):
\[
q_{\mathrm{local}} = Tq_{\mathrm{global}}, \qquad
k_{\mathrm{global}} = T^T k_{\mathrm{local}} T, \qquad
m_{\mathrm{global}} = T^T m_{\mathrm{local}} T .
\]

\subsection{Static Direct Stiffness Method}

The program assembles the global stiffness matrix \(K\) and force vector \(F\) by scattering element contributions into the global DOF map. For a bar or truss axial component,
\[
k = \frac{EA}{L}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -1\\
-1 & 1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
For frame members, the Euler--Bernoulli local stiffness matrix includes axial, shear, and bending coupling terms using \(EA/L\), \(12EI/L^3\), \(6EI/L^2\), \(4EI/L\), and \(2EI/L\).

Boundary conditions partition the static system into free and restrained DOFs:
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
K_{ff} & K_{fr}\\
K_{rf} & K_{rr}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
u_f\\
u_r
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
F_f\\
F_r
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
With prescribed support displacement \(u_r\), the solved system is
\[
K_{ff}u_f = F_f - K_{fr}u_r .
\]
After recovering the full displacement vector, reactions are obtained from
\[
R = Ku - F .
\]
Member-end forces are recovered in local coordinates from element displacements and fixed-end effects:
\[
f_{\mathrm{local}} = k_{\mathrm{local}}u_{\mathrm{local}} + fef_{\mathrm{local}} .
\]
The post-processor then exposes axial force \(N\), shear force \(V\), and bending moment \(M\) diagram data.

\subsection{Modal Analysis}

Modal analysis solves the generalized eigenvalue problem
\[
K\phi = \lambda M\phi,
\]
where \(\lambda = \omega^2\), \(\omega\) is circular frequency, \(f=\omega/(2\pi)\), and \(T_p=1/f\). Mode shapes are mass-normalized so that
\[
\phi_n^T M \phi_n = 1 .
\]

For influence vector \(r\), the modal participation factor is
\[
\Gamma_n = \frac{\phi_n^T M r}{\phi_n^T M \phi_n}.
\]
For mass-normalized modes this reduces to \(\Gamma_n=\phi_n^TMr\). Effective modal mass and participation ratio are computed as
\[
M_{\mathrm{eff},n} = \Gamma_n^2(\phi_n^T M \phi_n), \qquad
\rho_n = \frac{M_{\mathrm{eff},n}}{r^TMr}.
\]

Before modal solution, disconnected zero-mass DOFs may be removed, but stiffness-coupled massless DOFs are statically condensed:
\[
K_{\mathrm{eff}} = K_{aa} - K_{am}K_{mm}^{-1}K_{ma}, \qquad
M_{\mathrm{eff}} = M_{aa}.
\]
This preserves stiffness transfer while avoiding invalid dynamic DOFs with no participating mass.
