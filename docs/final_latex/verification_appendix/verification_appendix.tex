\section{Detailed Theory Notes}

This section retains the longer equations and implementation notes removed from the main report to keep the submitted main body within the CE 4011 page limit.

For a two-dimensional frame member with local axial and flexural behavior, the local stiffness contribution has the standard direct-stiffness form
\[
k_\ell =
\begin{bmatrix}
EA/L & 0 & 0 & -EA/L & 0 & 0\\
0 & 12EI/L^3 & 6EI/L^2 & 0 & -12EI/L^3 & 6EI/L^2\\
0 & 6EI/L^2 & 4EI/L & 0 & -6EI/L^2 & 2EI/L\\
-EA/L & 0 & 0 & EA/L & 0 & 0\\
0 & -12EI/L^3 & -6EI/L^2 & 0 & 12EI/L^3 & -6EI/L^2\\
0 & 6EI/L^2 & 2EI/L & 0 & -6EI/L^2 & 4EI/L
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
Truss members use the axial subspace of this relationship. Each element contribution is transformed into global coordinates, assembled into the global stiffness matrix, and partitioned into free and restrained degrees of freedom. With restrained displacement vector \(u_r\), the solved static system is
\[
K_{ff}u_f=F_f-K_{fr}u_r,\qquad R=Ku-F.
\]
Element end forces are recovered by returning the solved global displacement vector to local member coordinates and adding equivalent nodal-load effects where member loads, settlement, or temperature loads apply. The same recovered local-force data supports axial-force, shear-force, and bending-moment diagram sampling.

The modal workflow solves the generalized eigenproblem
\[
K_d\phi_i=\lambda_i M_d\phi_i,\qquad \omega_i=\sqrt{\lambda_i},\qquad T_i=\frac{2\pi}{\omega_i},
\]
on the active dynamic degrees of freedom. Massless DOFs that are stiffness-coupled to retained inertial DOFs are statically condensed rather than deleted:
\[
K_c=K_{aa}-K_{am}K_{mm}^{-1}K_{ma}.
\]
Direct deletion is reserved for disconnected zero-mass DOFs. Mode shapes are normalized for display and for modal-property reporting. Modal mass, participation factor, effective modal mass, and participation ratio are computed from the retained mass matrix and the selected excitation direction.

\section{Temperature Benchmark}

The temperature benchmark verifies thermal fixed-end effects and transformation-sensitive force recovery. The XML input contains uniform and combined temperature loads on frame and truss members. The inclined truss members are especially useful because errors in local/global transformation, thermal force sign, or SAP2000 coordinate mapping create visible force mismatches.

The strict thermal benchmark checks displacements, reactions, and member-end force components. The automated strict test uses a displacement tolerance of \(1.0\times10^{-5}\), a force tolerance of 0.05, and a moment tolerance of 0.6 in the project units used by the benchmark.

\section{Modal Flexible-Beam / Shear-Frame Benchmark}

The modal benchmark verifies that the dynamic workflow preserves stiffness coupling while avoiding invalid massless dynamic DOFs. The benchmark uses a flexible-beam/shear-frame-style model, assembles the stiffness and lumped mass matrices, condenses stiffness-coupled massless DOFs where required, and solves
\[
K\phi=\lambda M\phi .
\]
The comparison checks the reduced or condensed stiffness matrix, eigenvalues, circular frequencies, periods, and normalized mode shapes against the stored reference solution.
